Biography

I have not always been interested in Web Development. My vocational journey can be described topically.

When I was six or so, I saw Free Willy in theaters. That began, I believe, my interest in Marine Biology.
My favorite animal became, if it was not already, orcinus orca, also known as the killer whale. I would express
this fascination and appreciation by drawing pictures, basing a school project on the animal, and pursuing Biology
over the other sciences. It was only in High School that I began to look into the practical implications of a career in
marine biology. Due to my interest in orcas, I looked into an occupation that would allow me to work with them: a marine
mammal trainer at SeaWorld. My interest in that occupation quickly waned when I read that the average salary for that position
was around $36,000.00.

Somewhat disillusioned with the idea of Marine Biology, but still interested in Biology in general., I declared a Biology major to avoid
a rumored general education default courseload. This served me well until I hit the wall in Chemistry, which I took 3 times, and pre-Calculus,
which I underperformed in. I switched majors to Political Science and graduated with a B.A. After an internship in state-legislature, I had little
to no interest in Political Science, I applied to one degree-relevant position before looking for any job that I could get. I worked briefly for a
credit-card processing company and later for a similar period in the field of Mental Health.

In childhood and early adolescence, I was an avid reader. Reading was a major source of the fiction I consumed while growing up. This likely prepared me
for an interest in theater. I started by participating in school and church productions. Upon entering high school, I chose Choir as my fine arts credit
which gave me the opportunity to participate in show choir numbers and a few musicals. At one point, I did land a role in the drama department’s production
of ‘You Can’t Take it With You’ as the IRS agent. At Middle Tennessee State University, I would be cast in ‘Pirates of Penzance’ at an off-campus black box theater.
Eventually, after some years, I did dip my feet in Atlanta’s film and acting scene. This is where I was able to observe someone with real drive pursuing a career in Film
and Television. This is when I was able to decide that I was not willing to work three jobs between acting classes and auditions. A mutual friend helped land me the job of
Order Management Specialist. It was there that I worked until deciding to pursue a Computer Science degree at Concordia University in Seward, Nebraska.
